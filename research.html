NYU/Bellevue Stress and Resilience Study

Purpose and Goals

The purpose of this study is to identify factors that influence peoples' feelings of long-term distress following a physical assault. While some people recover quickly from a stressful event, others are distressed by the event for longer periods of time.

The goal of this study is to find ways to predict who will be distressed by a stressful event for a long period of time, and who will recover within a shorter period of time. This is a necessary step towards finding the most effective ways to help people who are at risk for being distressed or depressed for long periods following a stressful life event.

By gathering a variety of information about your initial response to your event that brought you to Bellevue Hospital, we can use this information to understand why some people will be distressed for a long period following the event, while other people will recover quickly.

About the Study

Visit 1: Emergency Department (20 minutes) The first visit is brief and it would take place today, in the Emergency Department. We would ask you some standard questions about your immediate response to your injury or accident.

Visit 2: Phone Screen Interview (30 minutes) The phone interview will take place within approximately 7 days of your visit to the Bellevue Emergency Department. This can be done at home or in the hospital if you have not yet been discharged. During the phone interview, we will ask you questions about how you have been feeling and thinking since the time of the event that brough you to Bellevue. Your responses to these questions will determine if you meet the final requirements to take part in the study.

Visit 3: One Month after ED Visit (4 hours) This visit will take place within appropriately 30 days of your visit to the Bellevue Emergency Department. There are several different components to the one month visit and the visit will take place in a private room at One Park Avenue. Components include: two online assessments, an interview, Affective Regulation, and some non-invasive physiological tests.

Visit 4: Three Months after ED Visit (1 hour) This visit will take place 3 months after your visit to Bellevue Hospital. This visit includes an online assessment and can be done at home.

Visit 5: Six Months after ED Visit (1 hour) This visit will take place 6 months after your visit to Bellevue Hospital. This visit includes an online assessment and can be done at home.

Visit 6: Twelve Months after ED Visit (1 hour) This visit will take place 12 months after your visit to Bellevue Hospital. This visit includes an online assessment and can be done at home.


Financial Compensation

In return for your time and effort, we will thank you for your participation. If found eligible at phone screening, you will be given $100 for completing assessment visit 3, $30 for completing visit 4, $30 for completing visit 5, and $40 for completing visit 6. After each assessment visit, a check will be mailed to you at the home address you give us at your initial assessment. The check is expected to arrive at your home address approximately 2–3 weeks after you have completed the assessment.

If you choose to leave or if you are withdrawn from the study for any reason before finishing the entire study, you will be paid for each visit you have completed.

If you complete all of the study visits, you will receive a total of $200 over the course of the study.

Privacy and Protection

Federal law required us to protext the privacy of any health information that identified you. This law is called the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA). We must keep all of your personal health information private unless you give us permission to share some of it. We will protect your confidentiality by storing any information from your medical chart in a file that does not include identifying information. Your study information will be coded with a subject code number unique to the study and that separates your identifying information from your study responses. All participants identifying information will be kept in locked and separate files. Research records are kept in a locked filing cabinet or stored digitally on a secure server.
